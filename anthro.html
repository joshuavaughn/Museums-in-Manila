Baybayin Gallery

The Baybayin Gallery highlights the ancient Filipino syllabic script through artifacts, archival records, and ethnographic collections. While replaced by the Roman alphabet, baybayin persists among Mindoro's Mangyan and Palawan's indigenous groups.

Palayok Gallery

The exhibit Palayok: The Ceramic Heritage of the Philippines explores the country's rich ceramic tradition, from Neolithic pottery to contemporary creations. It showcases ceramics' cultural significance, diverse forms, and uses, from cooking pots to mortuary vessels.

Lumad Mindanao Gallery

This gallery highlights the material culture of 13 Lumad groups, showcasing their identity, ties with neighboring communities, and sustainable, ecology-based heritage. "Lumad," meaning "born from the earth," refers to non-Muslim, non-Christian indigenous groups in Mindanao.
